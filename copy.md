## What The System Helps You Do

### Section Headline
From device readings to useful insight.

### Section Copy
Glucose Monitor turns Eaglenos CGM data into a practical dashboard for day-to-day review. After linking a device by serial number, you can pull recent readings, review your glucose trend, and ask for a plain-language summary of what the data suggests.

### Feature Points

- Link your Eaglenos CGM device by serial number.

## Who It Is For

### Section Headline
Built for people who want clearer CGM feedback.

### Section Copy
This dashboard is designed for Eaglenos CGM users who want a simpler way to review their glucose data. It is useful for people managing diabetes or prediabetes, health-conscious users tracking metabolic response, and caregivers who need a clearer view of recent glucose movement.

### Current Focus
The current version is focused on a single-user patient dashboard. It helps one user connect one or more devices, review readings, and generate AI-assisted summaries from recent CGM history.

## Problems We Solve

### Section Headline
CGM data is valuable, but it is often hard to act on.

### Section Copy
A continuous glucose monitor can produce hundreds or thousands of readings. The problem is not a lack of data. The problem is knowing what changed, when it changed, and what deserves attention.

### Problem Points

- Raw readings are hard to interpret without charts and summary metrics.
- Glucose spikes and low events can be missed when reviewing numbers manually.
- Users may not know whether their time in range is improving or getting worse.
- Vendor reports can feel too clinical for everyday decision-making.
- Generic health advice does not always match the user's actual glucose pattern.

### Section Copy

- Readings over the last 14 days
- Mean glucose in mmol/L
- Time in range from 3.9 to 10.0 mmol/L
- Hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia event count

## AI Insights Copy

### Section Headline
Ask what your recent readings are showing.

### Section Copy
AI insights use the selected device's recent glucose history to produce a short, plain-language summary. The goal is to help users notice patterns such as frequent lows, post-meal peaks, overnight changes, or opportunities to improve time in range.

### Prompt Examples
